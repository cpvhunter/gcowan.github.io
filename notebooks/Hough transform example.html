Hough transforms (Ex 1)¶

Hough transforms are a way of representing an image such that the parameters describing arbitrary shapes (typically lines or circles) in the image can be easily determined. The image need not be 2D. This technique is sometimes used in particle physics experiments to determine the parameters of particle tracks, where the image is the map of "hits" that the particle leaves as is traverses the detector medium (i.e., such as ionisation trail in a silicon sensor).

def generateTrack( nHits ):
    theta = random.uniform(-np.pi, np.pi)
    rho   = random.uniform(-100*np.sqrt(2.), 100*np.sqrt(2.))
    #rho   = random.uniform(0., 10.)
    tmpx = random.sample(xrange(100), nHits)
    x = []
    y = []
    for i in tmpx:
        tmpy = (rho - i*np.cos(theta))/np.sin(theta)
        if tmpy < 100 and tmpy > 0:
            x.append(i)
            y.append(tmpy)
    return (x, y, rho, theta)

The accumulator is a 2D array that is used to keep track of where the lines intersect in the \((\rho, \theta)\) space.

Now loop over all of the points in each track and follow their position through the Hough space. Keep track of the maxima, so that we can apply a sensible threshold at the end.
